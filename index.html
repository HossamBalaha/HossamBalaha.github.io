Biography

Hossam Magdy Balaha, born in Egypt in 1993, is a Ph.D. Candidate and Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA) at the Bioengineering Department, Speed School of Engineering, University of Louisville (UofL), USA. He also serves as an Assistant Lecturer at the Computers Engineering and Systems Department, Faculty of Engineering, Mansoura University, Egypt. In addition to his academic roles, he is a Senior Full Stack Laravel Web Developer, AI Engineer, and Freelancer. Hossam earned his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from the Computers Engineering and Systems Department at Mansoura University. He has created a range of educational courses on YouTube, covering topics such as Artificial Intelligence (AI) techniques in digital histopathology and radiomics, web development, and the Internet of Things (IoT). His work includes the development of various web and mobile applications. He has actively contributed to numerous projects and competitions aligned with his fields of interest. Hossam's primary research interests include Medical Image Analysis, AI, Deep Learning (DL), Machine Learning (ML), Computer Vision (CV), Radiomics, Soft Computing, Web Development, and System Development. He has published several research papers, presented at conferences, and contributed to book chapters on these topics. He has been recognized by the University of Louisville with several awards, including the Outstanding International Student Award. Additionally, Hossam serves as a reviewer for several prestigious journals, including IEEE Access, IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics, the Journal of Ambient Intelligence and Humanized Computing (JAIHC), and Multimedia Tools and Applications (MTAP).

Long Biography

Hossam Magdy Balaha, born in Egypt in 1993, is a dedicated scholar whose academic journey reflects his profound commitment to advancing the fields of Bioengineering, Computer Science, and Artificial Intelligence (AI). Currently, he is pursuing a Ph.D. and serving as a Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA) at the Bioengineering Department, J.B. Speed School of Engineering, University of Louisville (UofL), USA. In addition, Hossam holds the position of Assistant Lecturer at Mansoura University, Egypt.

As a GTA, Hossam actively contributes to teaching and mentorship, focusing on key areas such as AI, Machine Learning (ML), Medical Image Analysis, Digital Histopathology, Radiomics, and Medical Image Processing. He supervises students and oversees graduation projects, fostering the development of future engineers and researchers. At Mansoura University, he teaches courses on Computer Programming, Computer Graphics, AI, Multimedia, and FPGA, broadening his impact on education.

Hossam's research contributions are substantial, with an H-Index of 20 and over 1,000 citations. He has published more than 40 research papers (e.g., peer-reviewed articles, conference papers, and book chapters) across a wide array of topics, including Medical Image Analysis, Deep Learning, Digital Histopathology, Radiomics, Web Development, Internet of Things (IoT), Computer Vision, Cloud Computing, and Soft Computing. His ongoing research also explores the integration of AI into personalized medicine, multi-modal medical data fusion, and improving interpretability in AI models, particularly for medical applications.

Hossam's work has been widely recognized through multiple prestigious awards, including the Exemplary Research Scholarship Award (2023 and 2024), the Outstanding International Student Award (2024), the Inclusive Excellence Award (Finalist, 2024), and the Best Graduate Student Peer-Reviewed Journal Paper Award (2024). In addition, he received a Certificate of Recognition for attending PLAN Workshops (2024).

Hossam earned his Master's degree in Computer Engineering and Control Systems from Mansoura University with a perfect GPA of 4.00/4.00. His research for this degree focused on developing a Deep Learning Framework Model for Arabic Handwritten Text Recognition. He also holds a Bachelor's degree in Computers and Systems Engineering from the same institution, demonstrating his consistent academic excellence.

In addition to his academic roles, Hossam is a Senior Full Stack Laravel Web Developer, AI Engineer, and Freelancer. He has created several educational courses on YouTube, focusing on AI techniques in digital histopathology, radiomics, web development, and IoT. His professional work spans building systems, web, and mobile applications, and contributing to numerous projects and competitions aligned with his research interests.

Hossam's commitment to fostering inclusive excellence is evident in his active engagement with diverse cultural perspectives and his effort to integrate diversity and inclusion into his teaching. His bilingualism enhances his ability to communicate across cultures, and his participation in the NSF's Innovation Corps (I-Corps™) program underscores his commitment to incorporating diverse perspectives into entrepreneurial efforts.

A passionate advocate for social justice, Hossam supports initiatives addressing systemic issues like police brutality, White supremacy, systemic racism, and anti-Blackness. He is actively involved in community service, contributing to organizations like "International OASIS - Organization Assisting and Serving International Students and Scholars" and serving as a Graduate Student Ambassador for the University of Louisville. His dedication to service extends to regular blood donations and other volunteer efforts, exemplifying his values of empathy, compassion, and community engagement.

Through his academic, professional, and social initiatives, Hossam continues to make meaningful contributions to both his fields of expertise and society as a whole.

Part 1 of the GTA Academy, titled "Introduction to Teaching in Higher Education," is a semester-long series consisting of nine sessions conducted in the fall.
